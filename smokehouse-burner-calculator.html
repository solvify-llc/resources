What causes the gap?

Most smokehouses are commissioned to run reliably at high fire. The low-fire setpoint is often left higher than it needs to be because chamber airflow pushes the burner into instability at low load. The fix is an on-site combustion analysis, not a new burner.

📊

A real example

On a recent two-zone commercial food dryer job, the burners were idling at roughly 2.5x their 140,000 BTU/hr target. Solvify diagnosed the root cause as chamber perpendicular airflow, corrected it on-site, and delivered a prioritized improvement roadmap.
